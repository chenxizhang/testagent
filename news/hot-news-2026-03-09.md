## 🔬 Science & Space

### 11. Vera Rubin Observatory Sends 800,000 Alerts in a Single Night
**Category:** Science / Astronomy

The Vera C. Rubin Observatory, home to the world's largest digital camera, entered full scientific operations and sent out 800,000 real-time alerts in a single night, identifying supernovae, new asteroids, and other transient events. At full capacity, the observatory will issue up to **7 million alerts per night**, ushering in an unprecedented era of astronomical discovery.

---

### 12. NASA's Perseverance Rover Completes First Fully Autonomous AI Drive on Mars
**Category:** Science / Space Exploration

NASA announced that the Perseverance rover successfully completed its first fully autonomous AI-planned drive on Mars, navigating Martian terrain using onboard vision and mapping algorithms without human intervention. This milestone represents a major leap toward more ambitious robotic and future crewed Mars exploration.

---

### 13. AI Interprets Brain MRI Scans in Seconds — University of Michigan
**Category:** Science / Medical

Researchers at the University of Michigan have developed an AI system capable of interpreting brain MRI scans within seconds, with accuracy comparable to trained radiologists. The technology has the potential to revolutionize emergency care triage and early diagnosis of strokes and traumatic brain injuries.

---

### 14. Scientists Grow Chickpeas in Simulated Lunar Soil
**Category:** Science / Space Agriculture

In a significant step toward self-sustaining lunar colonies, scientists have successfully grown chickpeas in simulated lunar regolith (soil). The breakthrough brings humanity closer to producing food locally on the Moon, reducing dependence on costly Earth resupply missions for future lunar bases and long-duration space missions.
