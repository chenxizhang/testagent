## 🌐 World & Geopolitics

### 8. Strait of Hormuz Closure Triggers Historic Oil Supply Shock
**Category:** World / Energy  
The closure of the Strait of Hormuz amid an escalating conflict involving Iran, the United States, and Israel has caused the loss of nearly 20 million barrels of oil supply per day—the largest oil supply shock on record. Oil prices surged past $120 per barrel in Asian trading, sending global stock markets lower and raising stagflation fears worldwide.

---

### 9. Middle East Conflict Intensifies; UN Warns of Global Economic Risk
**Category:** World / Geopolitics  
The US-Israel-Iran conflict has deepened, with the United Nations issuing urgent warnings that the situation risks spiraling out of control. Civilian displacement in Lebanon is worsening, international shipping lanes are under threat, and world leaders are calling for de-escalation. The UN warns that the global economy faces a "grave risk" from prolonged instability in the region.

---

### 10. US Supreme Court Limits Presidential Tariff Authority
**Category:** World / Trade  
The US Supreme Court issued a landmark ruling limiting the President's authority to impose new tariffs unilaterally. While temporary global tariffs on imports remain in place, the decision has complicated ongoing trade negotiations with the European Union and India, and could reshape how trade policy is executed in the United States going forward.

## 🔬 Science & Space

### 11. Vera C. Rubin Observatory Begins Full Science Survey
**Category:** Science / Astronomy  
The Vera C. Rubin Observatory has launched its full science survey, producing up to 800,000 astronomical alerts per night—tracking supernovae, asteroids, and variable stars in real time. Astronomers expect the observatory to deliver a flood of new discoveries throughout 2026 and fundamentally transform how scientists study the changing universe.

---

### 12. Scientists Grow Chickpeas in Simulated Lunar Soil
**Category:** Science / Space Agriculture  
Researchers have successfully grown chickpeas in simulated lunar regolith, a key milestone in the development of sustainable off-Earth agriculture. The breakthrough brings humanity closer to building self-sufficient lunar bases that can grow their own food, a critical requirement for long-duration missions and permanent settlements.

---

### 13. Artemis II Crewed Lunar Mission on Track for 2026 Launch
**Category:** Science / Space Exploration  
NASA's Artemis II mission—which will send humans around the Moon for the first time since Apollo—remains on schedule for launch in 2026. Several commercial and international lunar landers are also set to deploy this year, including a new radio telescope designed to operate through the lunar night and expand our understanding of the early universe.

---

### 14. NASA's Perseverance Rover Completes First Fully AI-Planned Mars Drive
**Category:** Science / Space Exploration  
NASA's Perseverance rover has completed its first fully autonomous, AI-planned drive on the Martian surface. Artificial intelligence analyzed terrain data and plotted the route without any human intervention, demonstrating the growing role of automation in planetary exploration and paving the way for more autonomous future missions.
